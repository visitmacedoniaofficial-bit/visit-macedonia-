Why Macedonia

A small country that keeps surprising you.

Snow-capped mountains, a turquoise lake, Roman ruins, Byzantine frescoes and Ottoman bridges — all within a couple of hours' drive of each other.

Landlocked in the southern Balkans, Macedonia packs an unusual amount into a compact space. Mornings can start with coffee in Skopje's Old Bazaar — the largest in the Balkans outside Istanbul — and end watching the sun drop behind the mountains across Lake Ohrid.

The country runs on hospitality. Expect long lunches, homemade rakija, peppers roasted over open fire for ajvar, and hosts genuinely glad you came. It is affordable, walkable and refreshingly uncrowded.

Getting here by plane

Macedonia has two international airports. Skopje International Airport (SKP) is the main hub with the most connections, while Ohrid “St. Paul the Apostle” Airport (OHD) is the smaller second airport, mostly with seasonal summer flights for visitors to Lake Ohrid. A taste of Macedonia

Food & drink to try

From the national bean pot to Tikveš wine — flavours straight from the ground.

Tavče gravče

Macedonia's national dish — baked beans with peppers and paprika, served sizzling in an earthen pot.

Ajvar

A smoky relish of roasted red peppers, jarred every autumn in homes across the country.

Burek

Flaky filo pastry filled with cheese, meat or spinach — the classic morning treat.

Shopska salad

Tomato, cucumber, pepper and onion topped with a snowfall of grated white sirenje cheese.

Rakija

Strong homemade fruit brandy — the traditional drink to welcome a guest.

Vranec wine

Macedonia's signature red grape, deep and bold, at its best in the Tikveš valley.
